Arduino with P5JS!

Arduino and p5.js work together to create interactive design art by bridging the physical and digital worlds. Arduino collects real-world data through sensors like light, motion, or touch, and sends that information to p5.js via serial communication. In turn, p5.js, a JavaScript library for creative coding, uses the incoming data to generate real-time visuals, animations, or sounds in the browser. This interaction allows artists and designers to build immersive experiences where physical inputs directly influence digital outputs, such as changing colors, triggering sounds, or animating shapes on screen.

#1 Idea

Use sensors such as light sensors with Arduino to explore the cactus's metabolic system. For example, during the day, when light intensity is high, photosynthesis occurs‚Äîthis can be visualized in p5.js as a photosynthesizing cactus. In contrast, at night, when light intensity is low, the cactus engages in gas exchange.

#2 Idea

Measure participants‚Äô heartbeats using an Arduino sensor and transform the data into sounds or animations, inspired by Rafael Lozano-Hemmer‚Äôs Pulse Room.

Force Sensitive Resistor

This Arduino design uses a force-sensitive resistor (FSR) to detect pressure and light up a series of six LEDs based on how much force is applied. As the analog reading from pin A0 increases (due to more pressure on the FSR), the LEDs connected to digital pins 2 through 7 light up in sequence, creating a visual intensity meter. The code reads the FSR input and compares the value against thresholds to control which LEDs turn on, simulating a pressure-based level indicator. Useful for my ‚ÄúJay-An‚Äôs Social Distance Kits‚Äù where if someone touches my head, the light blinks to warn them.
